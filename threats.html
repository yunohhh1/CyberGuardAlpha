Common Cyber Threats

In today's highly connected world, cyber threats are becoming more frequent, complex, and damaging. Phishing is one of the most common attack methods where cybercriminals deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information through fake emails or websites. Malware, short for malicious software, includes viruses, worms, and Trojans that can damage systems, steal data, or provide attackers with unauthorized access. Ransomware takes things a step further by locking a userâ€™s data and demanding a ransom for its release. Meanwhile, social engineering manipulates individuals into disclosing confidential data by exploiting human psychology rather than technical hacking techniques. Understanding the nature of these threats is the first step in developing a strong defensive posture against them. Staying informed and cautious can drastically reduce your risk of falling victim to cyberattacks.
