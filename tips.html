Top Safety Tips

Protecting yourself online begins with practicing good cybersecurity hygiene. Always use strong and unique passwords for every account; consider using a passphrase or a password manager to help you remember them. Enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an additional layer of security by requiring a second form of verification, such as a text message or authenticator app. Keeping your software up to date ensures you have the latest security patches and bug fixes, which helps defend against known vulnerabilities. When browsing online, avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking or shopping. If necessary, use a trusted Virtual Private Network (VPN) to encrypt your connection. These simple yet effective practices significantly lower your chances of becoming a victim of cybercrime. Stay proactive, and make cybersecurity a daily habit.
